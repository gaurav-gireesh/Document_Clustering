Europe respected, both in peace and war, the majesty
of the Gothic throne. But the future happiness of
the queen and of Italy depended on the education of
her son, who was destined, by his birth, to support
the different and almost incompatible characters of
the chief of a Barbarian camp, and the first magi­
strate of a civilized nation. From the age of ten
years, Athalaric was diligently instructed in the arts
and sciences, either useful or ornamental for a Ro­
