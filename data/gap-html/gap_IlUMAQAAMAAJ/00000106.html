the court were strictly maintained. Every day, at
the appointed hours, the principal officers of the
state, the army, and the household, approaching the
person of their sovereign with bended knees and a
composed countenance, offered their respectful ho­
mage as seriously as if he had been still alive. From
motives of policy, this theatrical representation was
for some time continued; nor could flattery neglect
the opportunity of remarking that Constantine alone,
by the peculiar indulgence of heaven, had reigned
after his death *.
