After the death of Marcus, these liberal donations, and the
privileges attached to the thrones of science, were abo­
lished and revived, diminished and enlarged: but some
vestige of royal bounty may be found under the success­
ors of Constantine; and their arbitrary choice of an
unworthy candidate might tempt the philosophers of
Athens to regret the days of independence and poverty.1
