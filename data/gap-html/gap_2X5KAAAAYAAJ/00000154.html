and their auxiliaries. The Romans ad­

tians, overloaded with booty, were taken
by surprise. On a damp and slippery
soil, the swiftness of their horses was of no
use. Unable to retreat, they were cut to
pieces, more like men bound in fetter,
than soldiers armed for the field of battle.
