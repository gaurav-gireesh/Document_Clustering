The consuls knew well the extent of their commission
from the senate; but of all those bold declai'mers, who
had talked so high within the walls of the senate-house,
not one appeared to share with them in the public odium.
Besides, it appeared, that this contest with the people
would be a serious affair. Therefore, before they should
come to extremities, they judged it expedient to talk
over the matter with the senate a second time, which
they did accordingly. The young senators then crowd­
ed round the consuls chairs, ordering them to abdicate
the consulship, and resign an authority they wanted
spirit to support.
