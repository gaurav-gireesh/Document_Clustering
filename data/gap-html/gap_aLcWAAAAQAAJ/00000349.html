received above fifteen thousand pounds of gold 8s. This last was a
magnificent structure, erected by the emperor on the side of the
Quirinal hill, and dedicated, soon after the triumph, to that deity
whom Aurelian adored as the parent of his life and fortunes. His
mother had been an inferior priestess in a chapel of the Sun; a
peculiar devotion to the god of Light, was a sentiment which the
fortunate peasant imbibed in his infancy; and every step of his ele­
vation, every victory of his reign, fortified superstition by gra­
