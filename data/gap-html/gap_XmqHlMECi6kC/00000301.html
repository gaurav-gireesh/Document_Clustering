cast into a domestic chest in the custody of one of his
wives. Two years after the death of Mahomet, the sa­

and successor Abubeker: the work was revised by the
caliph Othman, in the thirtieth year of the Hegira; and
the various editions of the Koran assert the same mi­
raculous privilege of a uniform and incorruptible text.
In the spirit of enthusiasm or vanity, the prophet rests
the truth of his mission on the merit of his book, auda­
ciously challenges both men and angels to imitate the
beauties of a single page, and presumes to assert that
God alone could dictate this incomparable performance."
This argument is most powerfully addressed to a devout
