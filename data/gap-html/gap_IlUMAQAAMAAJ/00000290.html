were saved, to accompany, on carriages, the march of
the army, and to form occasional bridges for the
passage of the rivers. A supply of twenty days pro­

the rest of the magazines, with a fleet of eleven hun­
dred vessels, which rode at anchor in the Tigris, were
abandoned to the flames, by the absolute command
of the emperor. Their authority, of less weight,
