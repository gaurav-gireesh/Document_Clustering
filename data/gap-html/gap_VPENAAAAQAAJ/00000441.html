opprobrious word, were measured with scrupulous and
almost ridiculous diligence; and the prudence of the le­
gislator encouraged the ignoble practice of bartering
honour and revenge for a pecuniary compensation. The
ignorance of the Lombards, in the state of Paganism or
Christianity, gave implicit credit to the malice and mis­
