XLV. The victorious army halted at
the distance of five miles from Bedriacum.
The generals did not think it advisable on
the same day to attempt the enemy's camp.
Expecting a voluntary surrender, they
were willing to give their men some time
to repose. To encamp was not in their
power. The soldiers took the field, pre­
pared for battle, unincumbered, and of
course without the means of throwing up
entrenchments. Their arms and their vic­
tory were their only fortification. On the
