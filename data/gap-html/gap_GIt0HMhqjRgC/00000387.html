During ten successive days, the capital was assaulted
by the Avars, who had made some progress in the
science of attack; they advanced to sap or batter the
wall, under the cover of the impenetrable tortoise;
their engines discharged a perpetual volley of stones
and darts; and twelve lofty towers of wood exalted
the combatants to the height of the neighbouring
ramparts. But the senate and people were animated
