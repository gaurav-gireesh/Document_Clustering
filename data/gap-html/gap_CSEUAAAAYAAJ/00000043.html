rhetoric and philosophy maintained their superior reputation from the Peloponne-

possessed a pure air, a free navigation, and the monuments of ancient art. That
sacred retirement was seldom disturbed by business of trade, or government; and
the last of the Athenians were distinguished by their lively wit, the purity of their
taste and language, their social manners, and some traces, at least in discourse, of
