part of their diocesses is confined to the neighbourhood
of the Euphrates and the Tigris. The cities of Aleppo
and Amida, which are often visited by the patriarch,
contain some wealthy merchants and industrious me­
chanics, but the multitude derive their scanty sustenance
from their daily labour: and poverty, as well as super­
stition, may impose their excessive fasts,—five annual
lents, during which both the clergy and laity abstain
not only from flesh or eggs, but even from the taste of
wine, of oil, and of fish. Their present numbers are es­
teemed from fifty to fourscore thousand souls, the rem-
