sign of perpetual interdiction. Sion was deserted;
and the vacant space of the lower city was filled with
the public and private edifices of the JElian colony,
which spread themselves over the adjacent hill of
Calvary. The holy places were polluted with monu­
ments of idolatry; and, either from design or acci­
dent, a chapel was dedicated to Venus, on the spot
which had been sanctified by the death and resurrec­
tion of Christ. Almost three hundred years after
those stupendous events, the profane chapel of Venus
