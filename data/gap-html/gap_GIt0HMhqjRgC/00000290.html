him who is resolved on death; and his arm can only
be restrained by the religious apprehension of a fu­

the unfortunate, rather than the guilty; and the
poetical fables of the infernal shades could not se­
riously influence the faith or practice of mankind.
But the precepts of the gospel, or the church, have
at length imposed a pious servitude on the minds of
Christians, and condemn them to expect, without a
murmur, the last stroke of disease or the executioner.
