and declared their readiness to draw their swords in the service of
their oppressed country. It was the wish, and it soon became the
hope, of every citizen, that after expelling from Italy their foreign
tyrants, they should elect a prince who, by the place of his resi­
dence, and by his maxims of government, might once more deserve
the title of Roman emperor. The name, as well as the situation of
Maxentius, determined in his favour the popular enthusiasm.
