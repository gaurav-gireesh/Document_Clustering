tle. As soon as the Romans had landed on the

pursuit of the Barbarians; but, in a laborious march
of two hundred miles over the plains of Mesopo­
tamia, they endured the last extremities of thirst
and hunger. They were obliged to traverse a sandy
desert, which, in the extent of seventy miles, did
