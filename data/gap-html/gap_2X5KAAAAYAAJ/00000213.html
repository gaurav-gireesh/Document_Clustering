communicated his design. Claudius Phir-
ricus, who commanded the galleys on that
station, and Quinctius Certus, a Roman
knight, objected to the measure, and were
put to instant death. The rest of the as­
sembly, terrified by this act of violence,
swore fidelity to Vitellius. The populace,
as usual, blind and ignorant, but by con­
tagion catching the fears of others, fol­
lowed the example of the leading chiefs.
