"King ANTIOCHUS to ZEUXIS his father, sendeth greeting."

"If you are in health it is well. I also am in health. Hav-
"ing been informed that a sedition is arisen in Lydia and
"Phrygia, I thought that matter required great care: And
"upon advising with my friends what was fit to be done, it
"hath been thought proper to remove two thousand families of
"Jews, with their effects, out of Mesopotamia and Babylon,
"unto the castles and places that lie most convenient; for I am
"persuaded that they will be well disposed guardians of our
"possessions, because of their piety towards God, and because
"I know that my predecessors have borne witness to them, that
"they are faithful, and, with alacrity, do what they are desired
"to do-. I will, therefore, though it be a laborious work, that
"thou remove these Jews; under a promise, that they shall be
