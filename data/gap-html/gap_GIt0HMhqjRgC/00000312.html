dominion of a stranger: the youth was deservedly
popular; his name, after the death of Justin, had

own submissive offer of his head, with a treasure of
sixty thousand pounds, might be interpreted as an
evidence of guilt, or at least of fear. Justinian re­
ceived a free pardon, and the command of the eastern
army. The Persian monarch fled before his arms;
and the acclamations which accompanied his triumph
declared him worthy of the purple. His artful pa­
troness had chosen the month of the vintage, while
the emperor, in a rural solitude, was permitted to
enjoy the pleasures of a subject. On the first in­
