complaint of inofficious testament, to suppose that
their father,s understanding was impaired by sickness
or age; and respectfully to appeal from his rigorous
sentence to the deliberate wisdom of the magistrate.
Legacies. Jn the Roman jurisprudence, an essential distinction
was admitted between the inheritance and the legacies.
The heirs who succeeded to the entire unity, or to
any of the twelve fractions of the substance of the
testator, represented his civil and religious character,
asserted his rights, fulfilled his obligations, and dis­
charged the gifts of friendship or liberality which his
last will had bequeathed under the name of legacies.
But as the imprudence or prodigality of a dying man
might exhaust the inheritance, and leave only risk
and labour to his successor, he was empowered to
retain the Falcidian portion; to deduct, before the
payment of the legacies, a clear fourth for his own
