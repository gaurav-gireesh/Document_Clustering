captives, who generously broke their fetters, marched
through Bulgaria to the shores of the Euxine, de­
feated two armies of Barbarians, embarked in the
ships which had been stationed for their reception,
and returned to Constantinople; from whence they
were distributed to their respective homes. But the
freedom of Basil was naked and destitute: his farm
was ruined by the calamities of war: after his father,s
death, his manual labour or service could no longer
