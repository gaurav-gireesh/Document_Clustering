"You Romans speak with as many tongues, but they
"are tongues of deceit and perjury. To me you hold
"one language, to my subjects another; and the na-
"tions are successively deluded by your perfidious

"your master that a Turk is incapable of uttering
"or forgiving falsehood, and that he shall speedily
"meet the punishment which he deserves. While
"he solicits my friendship with flattering and hollow
"words, he is sunk to a confederate of my fugitive
"Varchonites. If I condescend to march against
"those contemptible slaves, they will tremble at the
"sound of our whips; they will be trampled, like a
"nest of ants, under the feet of my innumerable
"cavalry. I am not ignorant of the road which
"they have followed to invade your empire; nor
"can I be deceived by the vain pretence, that mount
"Caucasus is the impregnable barrier of the Ro-
"mans. I know the course of the Niester, the Da-
"nube, and the Hebrus; the most warlike nations
"have yielded to the arms of the Turks; and from
"the rising to the setting sun, the earth is my in-
"heritance." Notwithstanding this menace, a sense
of mutual advantage soon renewed the alliance of the
Turks and Romans: but the pride of the great khan
survived his resentment: and when he announced an
important conquest to his friend the emperor Mau­
rice, he styled himself the master of the seven races,
and the lord of the seven climates of the world *.
