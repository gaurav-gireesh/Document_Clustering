The Peloponnesians at Miletus had no sooner heard
of the recal of Alcibiades, than, as before they suspected
treachery in Tissaphernes, they, now loudly vented in­
vectives against him. What more inflamed them was,
that, ever since the Athenians shewed themselves before
Miletus and they had refused to put out to sea and en­
gage them, Tissaphernes had flackened more than ever
in his payments; and thus, hated by them for that
reason sufficiently before, he now became more odious
on account of Alcibiades. The soldiery again, as on
