Christians; and the tribute was increased to a slave,
a horse, and a thousand pieces of gold, for each of
the three hundred and sixty-five days in the solar
year. But as soon as the empire was again united
by the arms and policy of Abdalmalek, he disclaimed
a badge of servitude, not less injurious to his con­
science than to his pride: he discontinued the pay­
ment of the tribute; and the resentment of the
Greeks was disabled from action by the mad tyranny
of the second Justinian, the just rebellion of his sub­
