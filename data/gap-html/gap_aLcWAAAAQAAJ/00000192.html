His menaces inflamed the fury of the legion, and their brandished

"battle; me you may destroy, you cannot intimidate; and the

the emperor pronounced, with a loud voice, the decisive sentence,
"Citizens! lay down your arms, and depart in peace to your re-
"spective habitations.'' The tempest was instantly appeased; the
soldiers, filled with grief and shame, silently confessed the justice of
their punishment and the power of discipline, yielded up their arms
and military ensigns, and retired in confusion, not to their camp,
but to the several inns of the city. Alexander enjoyed, during
thirty days, the edifying spectacle of their repentance; nor did he
restore them to their former rank in the army, till he had punished
with death those tribunes whose connivance had occasioned the
mutiny. The grateful legion served the emperor whilst living, and
revenged him when dead 7i.
