such is the scarcity of wood, that some art is requisite

is destitute of navigable rivers, which fertilize the soil,
and convey its produce to the adjacent regions: the
torrents that fall from the hills are imbibed by the thirsty
earth: the rare and hardy plants, the tamarind or the
acacia, that strike their roots into the clefts of the rocks,
are nourished by the dews of the night; a scanty supply
of rain is collected in cisterns and aqueducts; the wells
and springs are the secret treasure of the desert; and
the pilgrim of Mecca," after many a dry and sultry

general and genuine picture of the climate of Arabia.
