The senate, in consideration of the services performed
by Publius Scipio, decreed a supplication for one day,
and then ordered Caius Laelius to return with all expe­
dition to Spain, with the ships which he had brought
thence. On the authority of a great many historians,
I have fixed the taking of Carthage in this year, al­
though I am not ignorant that several have placed it in
the year following; but it appeared to me very impro­
bable that Scipio should have passed a whole year in
Spain without doing any thing. [A. U. C. 543. B. C.
