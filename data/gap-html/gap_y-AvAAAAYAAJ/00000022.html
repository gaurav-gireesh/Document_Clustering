2. Now, Quintilius Varus was at this time at Jerusalem, be­
ing sent to succeed Saturninus as president of Syria, and was
come as an assessor to Herod, who had desired his advice in his
present affairs ; and as they were sitting together, Antipater
came upon them, without knowing any thing of the matter ; so
he came into the palace clothed in purple. The porters indeed
received him in, but excluded his friends. And now he was in
great disorder, and presently understood the condition he was in,
while upon his going to salute his father, he was repulsed by
him, who called him a murderer of his brethren, and a plotter
of destruction against himself, and told him that Varus should
be his auditor and his judge the very next day; so he found, that
what misfortune he now heard of was already upon him, with
the greatness of which he went away in confusion; upon which
