material disadvantage, which cannot even at present be totally over­
looked; a more expensive establishment, and consequently an in­
crease of taxes, and the oppression of the people. Instead of a
modest family of staves and freedmen, such as had contented the
simple greatness of Augustus and Trajan, three or four magnificent
courts were established in the various parts of the empire, and as
many Roman kings contended with each other and with the Persian
monarch for the vain superiority of pomp and luxury. The num­
