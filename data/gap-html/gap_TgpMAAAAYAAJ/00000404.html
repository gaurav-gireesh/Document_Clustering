* It was a usual thing for conquerors to change the
names of the persons they vanquished in war, in testi­
mony of their absolute power over them. Thus we find
the king of Babylon changing the name of Mattaniah into
Zedekiah, when he constituted him king of Judah,
2 Kings xxiv. 17. But our learned Usher has farther re­
marked that the king of Egypt gave Eliakim the name of
Jehoiakim, thereby to testify, that he ascribed his victory
over the Babylonians to Jehovah, the God of Israel, by
whose excitation, as he pretended, 2Chron. xxxv. 21, 22.
he undertook the expedition. Patrick's and Cahners
Commentary. B.

fro;n God; Meshach, in Chaldee, belonging to the god­
dess Sheshach; Azariah, in Hebrew, God is my help;

and Abednego, in Chaldee, the servant of Nego, i. e. the
