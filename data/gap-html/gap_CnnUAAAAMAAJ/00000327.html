5. However, Alexandra, the daughter of Hyrcanus, and wife
of Alexander, the son of Aristobulus the king, who had also
brought Alexander [two] children, could not bear this indig­
nity. Now this son was one of the greatest comeliness, and was
called Aristobulus; and the'daughter, Mariamne, was married
to Herod, and eminent for her beauty also. This Alexandra
was much disturbed, and took this indignity offered to her son
exceeding ill, that while he was alive, any one else should be sent
for to have the dignity of the high priesthood conferred upon
him. Accordingly she wrote to Cleopatra (a musician assisting
her in taking care to have her letters carried) to desire her in­
tercession with Antony, in order to gain the high priesthood for
her son.
