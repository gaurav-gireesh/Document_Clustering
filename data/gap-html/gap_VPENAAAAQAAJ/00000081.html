was sold at Rome for twelve ounces of gold; but the sup­

with the supply. If accident or monopoly sometimes
raised the value even above the standard of Aurelian,
the manufacturers of Tyre and Berytus were sometimes
compelled, by the operation of the same causes, to content
themselves with a ninth part of that extravagant rate.'
