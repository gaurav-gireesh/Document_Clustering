Alarmed by this intelligence, he hastily relinquished
the siege, and marched with rapid diligence from the
banks of the Tigris to those of the Oxus. The dan­
ger and difficulties of the Scythian war engaged him
soon afterwards to conclude, or at least to observe, a
truce with the Roman emperor, which was equally
grateful to both princes; as Constantius himself,
after the deaths of his two brothers, was involved, by
the revolutions of the west, in a civil contest, which
required and seemed to exceed the most vigorous
exertion of his undivided strength.
