confidence of the emperor. His passive and dutiful sub­
mission has been honoured with the praise of Justinian
himself, whose vanity was incapable of discerning how
often that submission degenerated into the grossest adu­
lation. Tribonian adored the virtues of his gracious
master: the earth was unworthy of such a prince; and

Romulus, would be snatched into the air, and translated
