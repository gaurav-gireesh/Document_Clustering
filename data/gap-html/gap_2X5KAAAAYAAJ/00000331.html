LXXXVIII. The fierce animosity that
broke out at Ticinum between the legions
and the auxiliaries, was not yet extin­
guished. Frequent quarrels occurred, and
ended always in mutual slaughter.
Against the peasants and farmers they
were sure to be unanimous, but agreed in
nothing else. The most dreadful carnage
happened within seven miles of Rome.
