caution had been used which could retard their pro­
gress, or defeat their design. The inhabitants, with
their cattle, were secured in places of strength, the
green forage throughout the country was set on fire,
the fords of the river were fortified by sharp stakes;
military engines were planted on the opposite banks,
and a seasonable swell of the waters of the Euphrates
deterred the Barbarians from attempting the ordinary
passage of the bridge of Thapsacus. Their skilful
