of his measures were compelled to acknowledge, that
he possessed magnanimity to conceive, and patience
to execute, the most arduous designs, without being
checked either by the prejudices of education, or by
the clamours of the multitude. In the field, he in­
fused his own intrepid spirit into the troops, whom
he conducted with the talents of a consummate ge­
neral; and to his abilities, rather than to his fortune,
we may ascribe the signal victories which he obtained
over the foreign and domestic foes of the republic.
