6. That Almighty God never permitted, in any one in­
stance, that such a human sacrifice should actually bo offered
to himself (though he had a right to have required it, if he
had so pleased,) under the whole Jewish dispensation, which
yet was full of many other kinds of sacrifices, and this at a
time when mankind generally thought such sacrifices of the
greatest virtue for the procuring pardon of sin, and the divine
favor. This the ancient records of the heathen world attest.

lator of Sanchoniatho. "4 It was the custom of the ancients,
