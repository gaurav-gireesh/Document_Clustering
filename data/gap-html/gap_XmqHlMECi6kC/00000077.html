would equally disappoint the prejudices of a Papist or a

Rome to intercept all correspondence with the Nesto-
rian patriarch, and several of his bishops expired in the
prisons of the holy office. The flock, without a shep­
herd, was assaulted by the power of the Portuguese,
the arts of the Jesuits, and the zeal of Alexes de Me-
nezes, archbishop of Goa, in his personal visitation of
the coast of Malabar. The synod of Diamper, at
which he presided, consummated the pious work of the
reunion, and rigorously imposed the doctrine and dis­
cipline of the Roman church, without forgetting auri­
cular confession, the strongest engine of ecclesiastical
torture. The memory of Theodore and Nestorius was
condemned, and Malabar was reduced under the domi­
nion of the pope, of the primate, and of the Jesuits, who
invaded the see of Angamala or Cranganor. Sixty
