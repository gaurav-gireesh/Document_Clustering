all those who might remember his former obscurity, or censure his
present conduct. He banished, or put to death, the friends and coun­
sellors whom his father had placed about him, to guide his inexpe­
rienced youth; and he persecuted with the meanest revenge his school­
fellows and companions, who had not sufficiently respected the future
majesty of the emperor. With the senators, Carinus affected a lofty
and regal demeanour, frequently declaring, that he designed to distri­
