inflamed the discontents of some inferior officers, and employed the
hand of Martialis, a desperate soldier, who had been refused the
rank of centurion. The devotion of Caracalla had prompted him
to make a pilgrimage from Edessa to the celebrated temple of the
Moon at Carrhæ. He was attended by a body of cavalry; but
having stopped on the road for some necessary occasion, his guards
preserved a respectful distance, and Martialis approaching his person
under a pretence of duty, stabbed him with a dagger. The bold
assassin was instantly killed by a Scythian archer of the Imperial
guard. Such was the end of a monster whose life disgraced human
