an army of Sclavonians fled before their march; but
within two days of their final departure, the designs
of Germanus were terminated by his malady and
death. Yet the impulse which he had given to the
Italian war still continued to act with energy and
effect. The maritime towns, Ancona, Crotona,

was reduced by the zeal of Artaban, and the Gothic
navy was defeated near the coast of the Hadriatic.
The two fleets were almost equal, forty-seven to fifty
galleys: the victory was decided by the knowledge
and dexterity of the Greeks; but the ships were so
closely grappled, that only twelve of the Goths
escaped from this unfortunate conflict. They af­
fected to depreciate an element in which they were
unskilled, but their own experience confirmed the
truth of a maxim, that the master of the sea will
always acquire the dominion of the land.
