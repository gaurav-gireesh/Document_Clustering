The entire series of Roman emperors, from the first
of the Caesars to the last of the Constantines, extends
above fifteen hundred years: and the term of dominion,
unbroken by foreign conquest, surpasses the measure of
the ancient monarchies; the Assyrians, or Medes, the
successors of Cyrus, or those of Alexander.
