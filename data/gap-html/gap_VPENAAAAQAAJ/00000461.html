a precedent and without a copy in the annals of the east.
The sonofNushirvan, who had requested to plead in his

assembly of the nobles and satraps." He was heard with
decent attention as long as he expatiated on the advan­
tages of order and obedience, the danger of innovation,
and the inevitable discord of those who had encouraged
each other to trample on their lawful and hereditary sove­
