of Greece were imitated by the royal colony of
Antioch. A stream of prophecy, which rivalled the
truth and reputation of the Delphic oracle, flowed
from the Castalian fountain of Daphne. In the ad­
jacent fields a stadium was built by a special privilege,
which had been purchased from Elis; the Olympic
games were celebrated at the expense of the city ; and
a revenue of thirty thousand pounds sterling was
annually applied to the public pleasures. The per­
petual resort of pilgrims and spectators insensibly
formed, in the neighbourhood of the temple, the
stately and populous village of Daphne, which emu­
lated the splendour, without acquiring the title, of a
provincial city. The temple and the village were
