poisoned by his brothers; and conjured his sons to
revenge his death, and to consult their own safety,
by the punishment of the guilty. Whatever reasons
might have been alleged by these unfortunate princes
to defend their life and honour against so incredible
an accusation, they were silenced by the furious cla­
mours of the soldiers, who declared themselves, at
once, their enemies, their judges, and their exe­
cutioners. The spirit, and even the forms of legal
proceedings were repeatedly violated in a promiscuous
massacre; which involved the two uncles of Con­
stantius, seven of his cousins, of whom Dalmatius
and Hannibalianus were the most illustrious, the Pa­
