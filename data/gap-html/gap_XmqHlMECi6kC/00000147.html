increase of taxes too often an indispensable duty. In
the use of his patrimony, the generous temper of Ni­

strictly applied to the service of the state: each spring
the emperor marched in person against the Saracens;
and every Roman might compute the employment of
his taxes in triumphs, conquests, and the security of
the eastern barrier.
