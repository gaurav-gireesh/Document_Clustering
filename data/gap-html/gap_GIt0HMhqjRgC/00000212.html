The fourth apparition, forty-four years before the
birth of Christ, is of all others the most splendid and
important. After the death of Caesar, a long-haired
star was conspicuous to Rome and to the nations,
during the games which were exhibited by young
Octavian in honour of Venus and his uncle. The
vulgar opinion, that it conveyed to heaven the divine
soul of the dictator, was cherished and consecrated
by the piety of a statesman: while his secret su­
perstition referred the comet to the glory of his own
times. The Jiflh visit has been already ascribed to
