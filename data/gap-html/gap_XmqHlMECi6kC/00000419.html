with his friend Caled, and the prophet of Medina enjoyed
at the same moment the satisfaction of embracing the
two firmest champions of his cause. The impatience of
Amrou to lead the armies of the faithful was checked by
the reproof of Omar, who advised him not to seek power
and dominion, since he who is a subject to-day, may be
