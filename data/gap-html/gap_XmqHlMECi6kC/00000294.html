as soon as he had disposed of the merchandize of Ca-
dijah. In these hasty and superficial excursions, the
eye of genius might discern some objects invisible to
his grosser companions; some seeds of knowledge
might be cast upon a fruitful soil: but his ignorance of
the Syriac language must have checked his curiosity;
and I cannot perceive in the life or writings of Maho­
