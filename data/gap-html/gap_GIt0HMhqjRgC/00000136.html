of the honour of victory; and as he could not hope
to establish his dominion, the Persian king displayed
in this inroad the mean and rapacious vices of a rob­

Chalcis, were successively besieged; they redeemed
their safety by a ransom of gold or silver, proportioned
to their respective strength and opulence; and their
new master enforced, without observing, the terms
and ruins of capitulation. No more than fourteen years had
elapsed since Antioch was ruined by an earthquake;
but the queen of the East, the new Theopolis, had
been raised from the ground by the liberality of Jus­
tinian; and the increasing greatness of the buildings
and the people already erased the memory of this re­
cent disaster. On one side, the city was defended
by the mountain, on the other by the river Orontes;
