On the festival of Christmas, the last year of the eighth
century, Charlemagne appeared in the church of St.
Peter; and, to gratify the vanity of Rome, he had ex­
changed the simple dress of his country for the habit
of a patrician/ After the celebration of the holy mys­
teries, Leo suddenly placed a precious crown on his
head," and the dome resounded with the acclamations
of the people,—Long life and victory to Charles, the
most pious Augustw, crowned by God the great and pa­
cific emperor of the Romans! The head and body of
Charlemagne were consecrated by the royal unction:
after the example of the Caesars he was saluted or
adored by the pontiff; his coronation-oath represents a
promise to maintain the faith and privileges of the
church; and the first-fruits were paid in his rich offer­
