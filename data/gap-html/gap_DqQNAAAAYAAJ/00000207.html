themselves. These stood in close order under cover
of their shields : Fabius, therefore, having heard of
the death of his colleague, ordered the squadron of
Campanian cavalry, in number about five hundred, to
fall back from the ranks, ride round, and attack the
rear of the Gallic line, sending the chief strength of
the third legion after these, with directions that wher­
ever they should see the enemy's troops disordered by
the charge, to follow the blow, and cut them to pieces
before they recovered from their consternation. After
vowing a temple and the spoils which might fall into
