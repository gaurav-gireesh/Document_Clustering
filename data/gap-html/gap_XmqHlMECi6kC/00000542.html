retreat exasperated the quarrel of the townsmen and
mercenaries; the guard of the gates and ramparts was
deserted; and, while they furiously charged each other
in the market-place, they were surprised and destroyed
by the sword of a common enemy. The male sex was
exterminated by the sword; ten thousand youths were
led into captivity: the weight of the precious spoil ex­
ceeded the strength and number of the beasts of burden;
the superfluous remainder was burnt; and, after a li­
