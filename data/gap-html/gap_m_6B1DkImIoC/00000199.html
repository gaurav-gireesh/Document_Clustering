JLT was with some difficulty that Quinctius composed
the people, but in bringing the other consul to himself,
the senate found still more. At length when the assem­
bly of the people was dismissed, the senate was conven­
ed by the consuls. Anger and fear dictating by turns,
produced a variety of sentiments; but the longer they
deliberated, the more they saw the folly of continuing
these contests; insomuch, that Quinctius had the thanks
of the house for interfering so seasonably, to suspend the
daemon of discord. On the other hand, they conjured
