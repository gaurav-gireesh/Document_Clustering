paid the forfeit of his neglect. But the ingenuity
of the civilians successfully laboured to convert
simple engagements into the form of solemn stipula­
tions. The praetors, as the guardians of social faith,
admitted every rational evidence of a voluntary and
deliberate act, which in their tribunal produced an
equitable obligation, and for which they gave an ac­
