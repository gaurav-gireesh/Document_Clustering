The partition of the lands of Italy, of which Theodo-
ric assigned the third part to his soldiers, is honourably
arraigned as the sole injustice of his life. And even this
act may be fairly justified by the example of Odoacer,
the rights of conquest, the true interest of the Italians,
and the sacred duty of subsisting a whole people, who,
on the faith of the promises, had transported themselves
into a distant land.0 Under the reign of Theodoric, and
