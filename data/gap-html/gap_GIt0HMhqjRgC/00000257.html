rulers. Yet the magistrates of Justinian were not
subject to the authority of the church: the emperor
consulted the unbelieving civilians of antiquity, and
the choice of matrimonial laws in the Code and Pan­
dects is directed by motives of justice, policy, and
the natural freedom of both sexes *.
