In this silken web, the weakness" of Constantine was

of Europe; but Asia was oppressed by two veteran
generals, Phocas and Sclerus, who, alternately friends
and enemies, subjects and rebels, maintained their inde­
pendence, and laboured to emulate the example of suc­
cessful usurpation. Against these domestic enemies, the
son of Romanus first drew his sword, and they trembled
in the presence of a lawful and high-spirited prince.
