of merit, and the friendship of the father always ensured the aver­
sion of the son. Suspicion was equivalent to proof. Trial to
condemnation. The execution of a considerable senator, was at­
tended with the death of all who might lament or revenge his fate;
and when Commodus had once tasted human blood, he became
incapable of pity or remorse.
