By their lofty claim of superior sanctity, the Jews might provoke
the Polytheists to consider them as an odious and impure race. By

contempt. The laws of Moses might be for the most part frivolous

large society, his followers were justified by the example of mankind;
and it was universally acknowledged, that they had a right to prac­
tise what it would have been criminal in them to neglect. But
this principle which protected the Jewish synagogue, afforded not
any favour or security to the primitive church. By embracing the
