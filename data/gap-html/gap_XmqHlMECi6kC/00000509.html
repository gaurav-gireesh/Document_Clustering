detection of error than for the investigation of truth, it is
not surprising that new generations of masters and dis­
ciples should still revolve in the same circle of logical
argument. The mathematics are distinguished by a
peculiar privilege, that, in the course ages, they may
always advance, and can never recede. But the ancient
geometry, if I and not misinformed, was resumed in the
same state by the Italians of the fifteenth century; and
whatever may be the origin of the name, the science of
