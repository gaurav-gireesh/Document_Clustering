of Trajan and the Antonines were united by laws, and adorned by

delegated authority; but the general principle of government was
wife, simple, and beneficent. They enjoyed the religion of their
ancestors, whilst in civil honours and advantages they were exalted)
by just degrees, to an equality with their conquerors.
