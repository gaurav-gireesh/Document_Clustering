He excelled in the private virtues of chastity and tempe­

His repasts were short and frugal: on solemn fasts, he
contented himself with water and vegetables; and such
was his strength as well as fervour, that he frequently
passed two days and as many nights without tasting any
food. The measure of his sleep was not less rigorous:
after the repose of a single hour, the body was awakened
by the soul, and, to the astonishment of his chamberlains,
Justinian walked or studied till the morning light. Such
restless application prolonged his time for the acquisition
